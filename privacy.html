Privacy Policy for JotSpace

Last updated: May 10, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes Our policies and procedures on the collection, use, and disclosure of Your information when You use the JotSpace mobile application (the "Service"), and tells You about Your privacy rights and how the law protects You.

We use Your Personal Data to provide and improve the Service. By using the Service, You agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

Quick Summary

JotSpace is designed to be local-first and privacy-respecting:

  • Your notes, journals, expenses, recipes, and personal content are stored on Your device by default and are encrypted at rest with AES-256-GCM.
  • Direct messages and group-chat messages are end-to-end encrypted on Your Device before being written to Firebase. The Company, Google, and Firebase staff cannot read message bodies.
  • The developer does not sell, rent, or share Your Personal Data with advertisers, data brokers, or marketing networks.
  • JotSpace contains no advertising SDKs and performs no behavioural advertising tracking.
  • Cloud backups are optional. When enabled, encrypted backups are written only to Your own Google Drive or Microsoft OneDrive account.
  • Social, chat, and multiplayer features require an account and rely on Google Firebase services described below.
  • You can sign out, clear local data, delete Your account, and request data deletion at any time (see "Your Rights and Choices" below, or visit Our public account-deletion page).

Detailed Description of Data Collection and Use

1. Account Information (Google Sign-In and Firebase Authentication)

Social, chat, and multiplayer features (friends, direct messages, group chat, announcement boards, Quiz Hub host, Forms, JotSplit, Study Group, etc.) require an Account. To create the Account, the Service uses Firebase Authentication with Google Sign-In. When You sign in:

  • Google shares Your email address and the display name from Your Google profile with the Application.
  • Firebase issues You a unique user ID ("uid") that the Application uses to identify You internally.
  • You then choose a public username and may optionally set a display name, short bio, and avatar emoji. These public profile fields are visible to other JotSpace users (in particular, anyone You add as a friend or share a multiplayer session with).
  • If You choose to upload a profile picture, the image is stored in Your own Google Drive (in the JotSpace app folder, accessed via the drive.file per-app scope) and a publicly-readable Drive link is shared in Your public profile so that friends can load it. Loading a friend's avatar requires Your Device to make an HTTPS request to a Drive URL controlled by Google; this discloses Your Device's IP address to Google in the same way as any other Drive image fetch.

You can use the core note-taking and personal Folio features without signing in. Only the social, chat, and multiplayer features require an Account.

2. Local User Content and Encryption at Rest

The core purpose of JotSpace is to let You create personal content — notes, journals, prayer entries, sermon notes, voice notes, recipes, expenses, splits, attached photos, file attachments, reminders, songbook setlists, study notes, and similar items. By default this content:

  • Is stored on Your Device in a local database (Room / SQLite) and the Application's private file storage.
  • Is encrypted at rest using AES-256-GCM. Long-lived encryption keys (the per-device "vault key", the database key, the per-message identity keys for end-to-end encrypted chat, and similar secrets) are wrapped by an Android Keystore master key. Where Your Device hardware supports it, the master key is additionally StrongBox-backed (a tamper-resistant secure element separate from the main CPU). Where Your Device requires it (for example, when deriving a key from a passphrase), key derivation uses Argon2id.
  • The Company has no access to any of these keys. Locally encrypted content cannot be decrypted by anyone except You on Your own Device.
  • Does not leave Your Device unless You actively share it with another user via a social feature, or You enable an optional cloud backup.

3. Optional Cloud Backups (Google Drive and Microsoft OneDrive)

JotSpace offers optional, user-initiated backups to Google Drive or Microsoft OneDrive. When You enable a backup:

  • The Application requests OAuth access to Your Google account using the drive.file scope, or to Your Microsoft account using the Files.ReadWrite + offline_access scopes. The drive.file scope is per-app: it grants access only to files the Application itself creates, not Your other Drive contents.
  • Your notes and attachments are encrypted by the Application before upload, using the on-device vault key, and stored as an encrypted archive in an Application-managed folder inside Your own cloud account.
  • Access tokens are stored only on Your Device, in hardware-backed encrypted storage. The Company never receives, stores, or proxies Your Google or Microsoft credentials, OAuth tokens, or backup contents.
  • You can disconnect the cloud account, delete the cloud backup file, or revoke the OAuth grant at any time from within the Application Settings, or from Your Google / Microsoft account dashboard.

Backup PIN and Cross-Device Recovery

Cloud backups are encrypted with a per-device vault key. To recover Your backup on a new device, You set a 6-digit backup PIN, which wraps the vault key into a small recovery blob that travels alongside the encrypted backup file. The PIN is never sent to the Company or to any third party. After 5 wrong PIN attempts, recovery is locked for 1 hour. If You lose the PIN, neither the Company nor Google can recover Your backup — this is a deliberate property of the design.

Google API Services User Data Policy — Limited Use Disclosure

JotSpace's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically, JotSpace uses the Google Drive API and Google Sign-In API only:

  • To allow You to authenticate to JotSpace using Your Google account.
  • To allow You to optionally back up and restore Your encrypted JotSpace data to a folder in Your own Google Drive.
  • To allow You to optionally upload chat-attachment images and profile pictures to Your own Google Drive so they can be shared via link with recipients You choose.

JotSpace does not transfer Google user data to third parties for advertising, market research, or building user profiles. JotSpace does not allow humans to read Your Google user data, except (a) with Your explicit permission, (b) for security purposes such as investigating abuse, (c) to comply with applicable law, or (d) where the data has been aggregated and is used for internal operations in accordance with the Limited Use policy. JotSpace does not use Google user data to train, improve, or develop generalized AI or machine-learning models.

4. Social Features, Chat, and Multiplayer (Firebase Realtime Database)

The Application uses Google Firebase Realtime Database as the backend for its social, chat, and live-multiplayer features. The data written to Realtime Database includes:

  • Public profile: Your username, display name, optional bio (≤140 characters), optional avatar emoji or avatar-image URL.
  • Social graph: friend requests, friend list entries, group memberships.
  • Direct and group messages (end-to-end encrypted): only ciphertext is written to Firebase. See "End-to-End Encrypted Messaging" below.
  • Announcement Boards / Community posts: posts, comments, polls, reactions, attachments. These are not end-to-end encrypted; they are encrypted in transit by TLS and at rest by Google, but Firebase staff have technical access to the post content.
  • Live presence: online status and "last seen" timestamps, which are visible to friends and other participants in shared sessions.
  • Multiplayer session data (Quiz Hub, Forms, etc.): nicknames, answers, scores, host metadata.
  • FCM token: Your Firebase Cloud Messaging registration token, written to /fcmTokens/{uid} so that Cloud Functions can deliver push notifications.

Realtime Database is hosted by Google on Google Cloud infrastructure; data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest by Google. Per the Firebase region for this project (Realtime Database, free-tier default), Your data is processed primarily on infrastructure in the United States. International transfers are addressed under "International Transfers" below.

End-to-End Encrypted Messaging

Direct messages and group-chat messages are end-to-end encrypted on Your Device before being written to Firebase. Plaintext message bodies and attachment payloads never leave Your Device unencrypted. Identity keys used for end-to-end encryption are generated and stored on each user's own Device, wrapped by the Android Keystore. Group keys are exchanged among group members and stored only on members' Devices.

As a result of this design, the Company, Google, and Firebase staff cannot read message bodies. Only You and the recipient(s) — using the identity keys generated and stored on Your respective Devices — can decrypt them. Some routing metadata is, however, necessarily visible to Firebase: sender and recipient user IDs, message timestamps, message size, group membership lists, read receipts, typing indicators, and reactions. Message bodies are not.

5. Push Notifications (Firebase Cloud Messaging)

The Application uses Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) to deliver push notifications for chat messages, reminders, multiplayer events, and support replies. FCM uses a per-installation registration token to route notifications to Your specific Device install. Push payloads for end-to-end encrypted chats contain only routing metadata (sender ID, conversation ID, ciphertext length); the message body is decrypted on Your Device when You open the conversation. You can disable notifications system-wide via Android settings, and disconnect the FCM token by signing out and uninstalling the Application.

6. In-App Support Chat

The Application includes an in-app support chat. To allow You to send support messages and receive replies without creating a paid account, the Application uses an app-scoped Android identifier (a per-app, per-device value provided by the operating system that changes when You clear the app's data or reinstall the Application) as the conversation key, and stores Your messages in Firebase Realtime Database under /support/{androidId}. Support messages and the identifier are used solely to operate the support feature. You can clear Your local support history from Settings at any time. Server-side support data may be retained for up to 24 months for issue-resolution and abuse-prevention purposes, after which it is deleted or anonymised.

7. Optional AI Features (Groq, Tavily, Serper) and AI Content Reporting

JotSpace integrates optional AI chat and web-search features powered by third-party APIs:

  • Groq — large-language-model inference for AI chat, AI vision (image tagging in the Files plugin), and Voice Notes AI features (Whisper transcription, summary, smart rename, translation, and "ask about this recording").
  • Tavily and Serper — web-search providers that supplement AI chat with current information.

These features are off by default. To enable them, You must obtain Your own API key from the respective provider and paste it into the Application Settings. The Company does not host an AI proxy and does not see Your prompts or responses in normal use. When You use an AI feature:

  • The text You enter and the immediate context of Your conversation are transmitted directly from Your Device to the provider You configured, using Your API key.
  • For Voice Notes AI features specifically (Transcribe, AI summary, Smart rename, Translate transcript, Ask about this recording, or Auto-transcribe on stop), the recorded audio bytes and any derived transcript text are also sent directly from Your Device to Groq using Your own API key. Audio is sent only when You explicitly invoke one of these features (or enable Auto-transcribe on stop in Voice settings). The Application never transmits Voice Note audio bytes to any AI provider in the background.
  • The provider's privacy policy and data-processing terms govern how they handle Your prompt. We strongly recommend reviewing the privacy policies of Groq, Tavily, and Serper before enabling these features.
  • JotSpace does not use Your notes, cloud backups, or other personal content to train AI models. JotSpace does not train any AI model itself.

AI Content Reporting

Each AI-generated response in the Application carries a flag / thumbs-down control. If You believe an AI-generated response is inaccurate, inappropriate, harmful, or otherwise problematic, You can tap that control to send a report to Us. A report is submitted only when You explicitly tap the control, and only contains: the text of the AI response You flagged, the category You select (e.g. "inappropriate"), an optional free-text note You add, and Your Firebase user ID so We can follow up if needed. Reports are stored in Firebase Realtime Database under /aiFeedback and are reviewed by the developer to investigate AI quality and safety issues. AI prompts You did not flag are not sent to Us.

8. Weather Folio (Open-Meteo and Approximate Location)

The Weather Folio uses the public Open-Meteo API. To show local weather, the Application requests the approximate (coarse) location permission. With Your permission, the operating system provides a city-level latitude and longitude, which the Application sends to Open-Meteo solely to fetch a forecast. The location is used in-memory and may be cached on-Device to avoid repeated lookups; it is not sent to any Company-controlled server. You may search for cities by name instead of granting location, or simply not use the Weather Folio.

9. Camera, Photos, Microphone, and Other Permissions

The Application requests the following Android runtime permissions only for the features described:

  • Camera — for the in-house QR-code scanner (used to add friends and accept session invites) and for capturing photos to attach to a note or message. Camera frames for QR scanning are processed on-Device using on-Device ML Kit barcode scanning and are not sent to any server.
  • Microphone (RECORD_AUDIO, FOREGROUND_SERVICE_MICROPHONE) — only to record Voice Notes that You explicitly start. Audio is stored on the Device. While a recording is in progress, the Application runs a microphone foreground service that shows an ongoing notification in the system shade and keeps the recording alive if You navigate away from the Application or lock the screen. The recording ends when You tap the in-app stop button; dismissing the notification does not stop the recording.
  • Approximate location (ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION) — only used by the Weather Folio, as described above.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS) — to deliver reminder and chat notifications.
  • Exact alarms (SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM, USE_EXACT_ALARM) and boot-completed receiver — to fire reminder notifications at the time You scheduled them, including after a device restart. If You revoke exact-alarm access in system settings, reminders fall back automatically to inexact alarms (which may fire a few minutes later than scheduled).
  • Battery-optimization exemption (REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS) — optional; without it some manufacturers may delay or drop reminder notifications.
  • Biometric / Fingerprint (USE_BIOMETRIC, USE_FINGERPRINT) — optional; used only for the local app-lock feature. Biometric matching happens entirely on Your Device's secure hardware. The Application never receives Your fingerprint or face data.
  • Nearby Wi-Fi devices (NEARBY_WIFI_DEVICES, with the neverForLocation flag, on Android 13+) — used by the Service Runner Folio to discover nearby Cast / Wi-Fi-Display targets. The flag tells the operating system that the Application is not using Wi-Fi scan results to derive location.
  • Fine location (ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION, only on Android ≤ 12) — used solely on older Android versions where the Cast / Wi-Fi-P2P discovery the Service Runner Folio uses requires location permission. On Android 13 and later, no location permission is requested for this purpose.
  • All Files Access (MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, Android 11+) — required by the Files plugin to provide a complete on-device file manager (browsing, preview, copy / move / rename / delete, and attach-to-note). The Application uses this permission solely to perform file operations You initiate. Although the permission technically grants the Application the ability to read all on-device storage, the Application reads file content only at the moment You explicitly preview, attach, or operate on a specific file. The Application does not enumerate, hash, or transmit file metadata or content in the background. There is no remote indexing, no telemetry of file names or paths, and no cloud upload except for files You explicitly attach to a chat or note (which uses Your own Drive). You can revoke this permission at any time in Settings → Apps → JotSpace → All Files Access; the Files plugin will then show its permission-request screen until You re-grant.
  • Legacy storage (WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, only on Android ≤ 9) — used solely to save attachments You explicitly download to Your gallery on older Android versions where scoped storage is unavailable.

You can revoke any of these permissions at any time from Your device's Settings, in which case the related feature will stop working but the rest of the Application will continue to function.

Security of Your Personal Data

We use industry-standard measures to protect Your data, including:

  • TLS / HTTPS for all network traffic between the Application and Firebase, Google Drive, OneDrive, Open-Meteo, the Bible Brain API, Groq, Tavily, and Serper. The Application forbids cleartext (HTTP) traffic at the operating-system level via a Network Security Config.
  • Certificate pinning for Firebase, Google Cloud Functions, and Google API endpoints. The Application pins the SubjectPublicKeyInfo (SHA-256) of Google Trust Services intermediates and roots in the Network Security Config. A network attacker who has installed a malicious certificate authority on Your Device cannot intercept these connections.
  • Local encryption at rest using AES-256-GCM, with long-lived keys wrapped by the Android Keystore master key (StrongBox-backed where supported), and Argon2id for any passphrase-to-key derivation.
  • End-to-end encryption for direct messages and group-chat messages.
  • Encryption of cloud backups on the Device before upload.
  • Server-side access controls on Firebase Realtime Database via security rules that restrict each user's data to their own Account.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While We strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect Your Personal Data, We cannot guarantee its absolute security.

Your Rights and Choices

Account Deletion (How to Delete Your Account and Data)

You can request deletion of Your JotSpace Account and associated Personal Data at any time. There are three ways to do this:

  1. In the Application — Open Settings → Account → Delete Account, confirm the prompt, and the Application will sign You out and submit a deletion request. We will delete Your Firebase Auth record, public profile, friends list, FCM token, support chat, and any messages You authored within 30 days. Group-chat messages You sent may remain visible to recipients in their own copies of the conversation, but Your authorship pointer will be replaced with "Deleted user".
  2. From Our public deletion page — Visit our account-deletion page for a step-by-step guide that does not require having the app installed.
  3. By email — If You cannot access the Application, send a deletion request from the email address associated with Your Account to JotSpace@zohomailcloud.ca with the subject line "Account Deletion Request". We will verify Your identity and process the request within 30 days.

Account deletion does not delete content You have stored only on Your Device or backed up to Your own Google Drive or OneDrive. To delete on-Device content, uninstall the Application or use Settings → Storage → Clear All Data. To delete cloud backups, sign in to Your Drive or OneDrive account and delete the JotSpace backup folder, or revoke JotSpace's OAuth grant from Google Account permissions or the Microsoft App access page.
