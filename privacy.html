Privacy Policy for JotSpace

Last updated: May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes Our policies and procedures on the collection, use, and disclosure of Your information when You use the JotSpace mobile application (the "Service"), and tells You about Your privacy rights and how the law protects You.

We use Your Personal Data to provide and improve the Service. By using the Service, You agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this Privacy Policy.

Quick Summary

JotSpace is designed to be local-first and privacy-respecting:

  • Your notes, journals, expenses, recipes, and personal content are stored on Your device by default and are encrypted at rest.
  • The developer does not sell, rent, or share Your Personal Data with advertisers, data brokers, or marketing networks.
  • JotSpace contains no advertising SDKs and performs no behavioural advertising tracking.
  • Cloud backups are optional. When enabled, encrypted backups are written only to Your own Google Drive or Microsoft OneDrive account.
  • Social, chat, and multiplayer features require an account and rely on Google Firebase services described below.
  • You can sign out, clear local data, delete Your account, and request data deletion at any time (see "Your Rights and Choices" below).

Detailed Description of Data Collection and Use

1. Account Information (Google Sign-In and Firebase Authentication)

Social, chat, and multiplayer features (friends, direct messages, group chat, announcement boards, Quiz Hub host, Forms, JotSplit, Study Group, etc.) require an Account. To create the Account, the Service uses Firebase Authentication with Google Sign-In. When You sign in:

  • Google shares Your email address and the display name from Your Google profile with the Application.
  • Firebase issues You a unique user ID ("uid") that the Application uses to identify You internally.
  • You then choose a public username and may optionally set a display name, short bio, and avatar emoji. These public profile fields are visible to other JotSpace users (in particular, anyone You add as a friend or share a multiplayer session with).
  • If You choose to upload a profile picture, the image is stored in Your own Google Drive (in the JotSpace app folder) and a link is shared in Your public profile so that friends can load it.

You can use the core note-taking and personal Folio features without signing in. Only the social, chat, and multiplayer features require an Account.

2. Local User Content

The core purpose of JotSpace is to let You create personal content — notes, journals, prayer entries, sermon notes, voice notes, recipes, expenses, splits, attached photos, file attachments, reminders, songbook setlists, study notes, and similar items. By default this content:

  • Is stored on Your Device in a local database (Room / SQLite) and the Application's private file storage.
  • Is encrypted at rest using a passphrase generated and stored on Your Device in Android's hardware-backed encrypted storage (EncryptedSharedPreferences). The Company has no access to this passphrase or to Your local content.
  • Does not leave Your Device unless You actively share it with another user via a social feature, or You enable an optional cloud backup.

3. Optional Cloud Backups (Google Drive and Microsoft OneDrive)

JotSpace offers optional, user-initiated backups to Google Drive or Microsoft OneDrive. When You enable a backup:

  • The Application requests OAuth access to Your Google account using the drive.file scope, or to Your Microsoft account using the Files.ReadWrite + offline_access scopes. The drive.file scope is per-app: it grants access only to files the Application itself creates, not Your other Drive contents.
  • Your notes and attachments are encrypted by the Application before upload and stored as an encrypted archive in an Application-managed folder inside Your own cloud account.
  • Access tokens are stored only on Your Device, in hardware-backed encrypted storage. The Company never receives, stores, or proxies Your Google or Microsoft credentials, OAuth tokens, or backup contents.
  • You can disconnect the cloud account, delete the cloud backup file, or revoke the OAuth grant at any time from within the Application Settings, or from Your Google / Microsoft account dashboard.

Google API Services User Data Policy — Limited Use Disclosure

JotSpace's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. Specifically, JotSpace uses the Google Drive API and Google Sign-In API only:

  • To allow You to authenticate to JotSpace using Your Google account.
  • To allow You to optionally back up and restore Your encrypted JotSpace data to a folder in Your own Google Drive.
  • To allow You to optionally upload chat-attachment images and profile pictures to Your own Google Drive so they can be shared via link with recipients You choose.

JotSpace does not transfer Google user data to third parties for advertising, market research, or building user profiles. JotSpace does not allow humans to read Your Google user data, except (a) with Your explicit permission, (b) for security purposes such as investigating abuse, (c) to comply with applicable law, or (d) where the data has been aggregated and is used for internal operations in accordance with the Limited Use policy. JotSpace does not use Google user data to train, improve, or develop generalized AI or machine-learning models.

4. Social Features, Chat, and Multiplayer (Firebase Realtime Database)

The Application uses Google Firebase Realtime Database as the backend for its social, chat, and live-multiplayer features. The data written to Realtime Database includes:

  • Public profile: Your username, display name, optional bio (≤140 characters), optional avatar emoji or avatar-image URL.
  • Social graph: friend requests, friend list entries, group memberships.
  • Direct and group messages: message text, attachment links, timestamps, read cursors, typing indicators, reactions.
  • Announcement Boards / Community posts: posts, comments, polls, reactions, attachments.
  • Live presence: online status and "last seen" timestamps, which are visible to friends and other participants in shared sessions.
  • Multiplayer session data (Quiz Hub, Forms, etc.): nicknames, answers, scores, host metadata.
  • FCM token: Your Firebase Cloud Messaging registration token, written to /fcmTokens/{uid} so that Cloud Functions can deliver push notifications.

Messages You send to other users are visible to those users on their devices and remain on Firebase servers for delivery and synchronisation across Your devices. Realtime Database is hosted by Google on Google Cloud infrastructure; data is encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest by Google.

5. Push Notifications (Firebase Cloud Messaging)

The Application uses Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM) to deliver push notifications for chat messages, reminders, multiplayer events, and support replies. FCM uses a per-installation registration token to route notifications to Your specific Device install. You can disable notifications system-wide via Android settings, and disconnect the FCM token by signing out and uninstalling the Application.

6. In-App Support Chat

The Application includes an in-app support chat. To allow You to send support messages and receive replies without creating a paid account, the Application uses Your Android ID (a per-app, per-device identifier provided by the operating system) as the conversation key, and stores Your messages in Firebase Realtime Database under /support/{androidId}. Support messages and the Android ID are used solely to operate the support feature. You can clear Your local support history from Settings at any time. Server-side support data may be retained for up to 24 months for issue-resolution and abuse-prevention purposes, after which it is deleted or anonymised.

7. Optional AI Features (Groq, Tavily, Serper)

JotSpace integrates optional AI chat and web-search features powered by third-party APIs:

  • Groq — large-language-model inference for AI chat.
  • Tavily and Serper — web-search providers that supplement AI chat with current information.

These features are off by default. To enable them, You must obtain Your own API key from the respective provider and paste it into the Application Settings. The Company does not host an AI proxy and does not see Your prompts or responses. When You use an AI feature:

  • The text You enter and the immediate context of Your conversation are transmitted directly from Your Device to the provider You configured, using Your API key.
  • The provider's privacy policy and data-processing terms govern how they handle Your prompt. We strongly recommend reviewing the privacy policies of Groq, Tavily, and Serper before enabling these features.
  • JotSpace does not use Your notes, cloud backups, or other personal content to train AI models. JotSpace does not train any AI model itself.

8. Weather Folio (Open-Meteo and Approximate Location)

The Weather Folio uses the public Open-Meteo API. To show local weather, the Application requests the approximate (coarse) location permission. With Your permission, the operating system provides a city-level latitude and longitude, which the Application sends to Open-Meteo solely to fetch a forecast. The location is used in-memory and may be cached on-Device to avoid repeated lookups; it is not sent to any Company-controlled server. You may search for cities by name instead of granting location, or simply not use the Weather Folio.

9. Camera, Photos, Microphone, and Other Permissions

The Application requests the following Android runtime permissions only for the features described:

  • Camera — for the in-house QR-code scanner (used to add friends and accept session invites) and for capturing photos to attach to a note or message. Camera frames for QR scanning are processed on-Device using on-Device ML Kit barcode scanning and are not sent to any server.
  • Microphone (RECORD_AUDIO) — only to record Voice Notes that You explicitly start. Audio is stored on the Device.
  • Approximate location (ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION) — only used by the Weather Folio, as described above.
  • Notifications (POST_NOTIFICATIONS) — to deliver reminder and chat notifications.
  • Exact alarms (SCHEDULE_EXACT_ALARM, USE_EXACT_ALARM) and boot-completed receiver — to fire reminder notifications at the time You scheduled them, including after a device restart.
  • Battery-optimization exemption (REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS) — optional; without it some manufacturers may delay or drop reminder notifications.
  • Biometric / Fingerprint (USE_BIOMETRIC, USE_FINGERPRINT) — optional; used only for the local app-lock feature. Biometric matching happens entirely on Your Device's secure hardware. The Application never receives Your fingerprint or face data.
  • All Files Access (MANAGE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, Android 11+) — required by the Files plugin to provide a complete on-device file manager (browsing, preview, copy / move / rename / delete, and attach-to-note). The Application uses this permission solely to perform file operations You initiate. No file content is ever read in the background, transmitted to any Company-controlled server, indexed remotely, or used for advertising or analytics. You can revoke this permission at any time in Settings → Apps → JotSpace → All Files Access; the Files plugin will then show its permission-request screen until You re-grant.
  • Legacy storage (WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE, only on Android ≤ 9) — used solely to save attachments You explicitly download to Your gallery on older Android versions where scoped storage is unavailable.

You can revoke any of these permissions at any time from Your device's Settings, in which case the related feature will stop working but the rest of the Application will continue to function.

10. Mini-Apps, Folios, and Religious / Devotional Content

JotSpace includes built-in mini-apps and productivity tools collectively called "Folios" — including the Bible reader (with bundled audio timing data), Devotion Journal, Prayer Journal, Sermon Notes, Memory Kids, Memory Verse Trainer, Reading Plan, Songbook, Recipe Vault, Expense Tracker, JotSplit, Event Agenda, Forms, Quiz Hub, Service Runner, in-app Browser, PDF reader, the Files plugin (a complete on-device file manager — see Section 9 for the All Files Access permission it uses), and casual games (Mahjong, Snake, Tic Tac Toe). Content You create in these Folios is treated as Local User Content (Section 2 above). Some of this content is religious or devotional in nature. We do not infer or sell information about Your religion, beliefs, or practices, and we do not classify users on the basis of any such information.

11. Diagnostic Data

The Application writes runtime diagnostic logs to the standard Android system log (logcat) on Your Device. These logs remain on the Device and are not collected by the Company. JotSpace does not integrate Firebase Crashlytics, Firebase Analytics, Google Analytics, AppMetrica, Mixpanel, or any comparable behavioural-analytics SDK.

12. No Advertising and No Sale of Personal Data

JotSpace does not contain advertisements, advertising SDKs, or third-party tracking technologies. We do not sell, rent, or share Your Personal Data with advertisers, advertising networks, data brokers, or social-media advertising platforms.

Your Rights and Choices

Account Deletion (How to Delete Your Account and Data)

You can request deletion of Your JotSpace Account and associated Personal Data at any time. There are two ways to do this:

  1. In the Application — Open Settings → Account → Delete Account, confirm the prompt, and the Application will sign You out and submit a deletion request. We will delete Your Firebase Auth record, public profile, friends list, FCM token, support chat, and any messages You authored within 30 days. Group-chat messages You sent may remain visible to recipients in their own copies of the conversation, but Your authorship pointer will be replaced with "Deleted user".
  2. By email — If You cannot access the Application, send a deletion request from the email address associated with Your Account to JotSpace@zohomailcloud.ca with the subject line "Account Deletion Request". We will verify Your identity and process the request within 30 days.

Account deletion does not delete content You have stored only on Your Device or backed up to Your own Google Drive or OneDrive. To delete on-Device content, uninstall the Application or use Settings → Storage → Clear All Data. To delete cloud backups, sign in to Your Drive or OneDrive account and delete the JotSpace backup folder, or revoke JotSpace's OAuth grant from Google Account permissions or the Microsoft App access page.

  • Server-side access controls on Firebase Realtime Database via security rules that restrict each user's data to their own Account.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or method of electronic storage is 100% secure. While We strive to use commercially reasonable means to protect Your Personal Data, We cannot guarantee its absolute security.
